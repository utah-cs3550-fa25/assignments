# Funeral Potatoes
Tags: #comfort #holiday #utah
Photo: funeral-potatoes.png

Funeral Potatoes is a comforting casserole of tender hash browns bound
in a rich blend of sour cream, cream of chicken soup, melted butter
and savory seasonings, then studded with sharp cheddar. A buttery
corn-flake topping bakes up golden and crisp, adding a satisfying
crunch to each creamy forkful. Perfect for potlucks or family
gatherings, it’s an easy make-ahead crowd-pleaser.

Prep: 15 minutes
Cook: 45 minutes
Serves: 12

1. Thaw the hash browns on a baking sheet overnight if frozen
   - 2 lb frozen hash browns
2. Preheat over to 350 F
3. Combine sour cream, cream of chicken soup, 6 tbsp of melted butter,
   salt, pepper and dried onion. Mix well.
   - 2 cup sour cream
   - 1 can cream of chicken soup
   - 6 tbsp butter
   - 1 tsp salt
   - .25 tsp black pepper
   - 1 tsp dried onion
4. Add potatoes and shredded cheese and stir to combine. Spoon into
   baking tray, about one to two inches thick.
   - 2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
5. Crush corn flakes into powder using a large ziplock bag.
   - 2 cup corn flakes
6. Melt 4 tbsp of butter and add to corn flakes. Mix well, then spread
   over the potatoes.
   - 4 tbsp butter
7. Bake uncovered for 45 minutes, then serve hot

   - 1 can cream of chicken soup

# Green Jell-O with Pineapple and Carrots
Tags: #holiday #utah
Photo: green-jello-with-pineapple.png

Green Jell-O with Pineapple and Carrots is a vibrant retro dessert of
tangy lime gelatin studded with sweet pineapple tidbits and tender
carrot ribbons. Chilled until firm, it offers a playful contrast of
juicy fruit and mellow vegetable textures in every wobbling bite. An
easy, make-ahead treat that brightens potlucks and family gatherings
alike.

Prep: 15 minutes
Cook: 240 minutes
Serves: 8

1. Sprinkle gelatin over boiling water; let bloom 1 minute, then stir
   until fully dissolved.
   - 1 package lime gelatin
   - 1 cup boiling water
2. Stir in cold water and pineapple juice.
   - 1 cup cold water
   - .5 cup pineapple juice
3. Fold in drained pineapple and finely shredded carrot.
   - 1 cup canned pineapple tidbits, drained
   - .5 cup shredded carrot
4. Pour into a lightly greased mold or dish.
5. Refrigerate uncovered until set, about 4 hours, then unmold (if
   desired) and serve.

# Lion House Rolls
Tags: #holiday #utah
Photo: lion-house-rolls.png

Lion House Rolls are soft, pillowy dinner rolls enriched with milk, butter, and a hint of sweetness  
that bake to a golden brown crust. Their tender crumb and buttery flavor make them an ideal companion  
for soups, stews, or holiday feasts. Easy to prepare ahead, these rolls deliver bakery-quality results  
in your own kitchen.

Prep: 90 minutes  
Cook: 20 minutes  
Serves: 12

1. Warm milk and butter until about 110 F.  
   - 1 cup milk  
   - 0.25 cup butter  
2. Stir in yeast and sugar; let activate until foamy, about 10 minutes.  
   - 1 packet active dry yeast  
   - 0.25 cup granulated sugar  
3. Mix flour, salt, and egg in a large bowl; add yeast mixture and stir into a soft dough.  
   - 3 cup all-purpose flour  
   - 1 teaspoon salt  
   - 1 large egg  
4. Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead until smooth, about 8 minutes.  
5. Place dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled, about 60 minutes.  
6. Punch down dough, divide into 12 portions, shape into balls, and arrange in a greased 9×13 pan; let rise 20 minutes.  
7. Brush rolls with melted butter and bake at 375 F for 20 minutes, until golden.  
   - 2 tablespoon butter, melted

# Horchata
Tags: #latino

Horchata is a creamy, cinnamon-infused beverage made by blending soaked rice with water and sweetened with sugar and vanilla. Strained to a silky texture and chilled until cold, it offers a refreshing, subtly spiced flavor. Perfect for hot days or to accompany spicy dishes, this make-ahead drink is both easy and crowd-pleasing.

Prep: 15 minutes  
Cook: 0 minutes  
Serves: 6

1. Rinse rice until water runs clear, then combine with water and cinnamon stick in a bowl; soak 4 hours or overnight.  
   - 1 cup rice, rinsed  
   - 2 cup water  
   - 1 cinnamon stick  
2. Transfer rice mixture to a blender and blend until smooth.  
3. Strain mixture through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth into a pitcher, discarding solids.  
4. Stir in milk, sugar, and vanilla until sugar dissolves.  
   - 4 cup milk  
   - 0.5 cup sugar  
   - 1 teaspoon vanilla extract  
5. Refrigerate until cold, about 240 minutes.  
6. Serve over ice and sprinkle with ground cinnamon.  
   - 0.25 teaspoon ground cinnamon
